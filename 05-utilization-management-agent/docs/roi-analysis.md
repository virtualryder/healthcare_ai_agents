## Why payers fund UM automation — and why governance is the gate
| Driver | Note | Source class |
|---|---|---|
| Plans prioritizing agentic AI for UM/PA/claims | 70% | industry-research (Deloitte 2025) |
| Regulatory scrutiny of algorithmic UM | CMS: AI must not make MA coverage decisions; a human determines | gov |
| Nurse-review time per case | High; criteria application + evidence assembly are the cost | sector-press |

The value is faster, more consistent criteria application and evidence summarization for the
UM nurse — **not** automated denials. The withheld-determination control and four-fifths
fairness screen are what let a compliance and legal team approve the deployment; without them
the ROI is unrealizable because the program is not defensible.

## Value model (fill per customer)
```
UM reviews per month                      U
Nurse minutes per review                  m   (e.g. 25)
Agent deflection of criteria/summary work e   (e.g. 0.45)
Loaded nurse cost per hour                c

Nurse hours returned/month ≈ U * m/60 * e
Annualized capacity recovered ≈ (monthly hours) * 12 * c
```
Secondary value: more consistent criteria application (fewer overturned determinations on
appeal) and an auditable, fairness-screened record for regulators.

## What is NOT claimed
The agent does not issue determinations, deny coverage, or take adverse action. It recommends;
a medical director decides. Value is measured as recovered nurse capacity and review
consistency in a pilot.
